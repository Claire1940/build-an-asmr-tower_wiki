<Section icon={Shield} title="Essential Components for an asmr tower build">

To build a stable and enjoyable tower, you must understand the interaction between aesthetic keys and structural blocks. Many decorative items, such as custom keyboard paths, lack native solid collision boundaries. If a player steps directly onto a keyboard block without support, they will fall straight through. Therefore, layering solid, invisible, or standard platforms directly beneath your custom keys is the foundational rule of any successful layout.

In addition to basic platforms, incorporating specialized blocks can dramatically improve the pacing of your climb. Bouncy blocks, disappearing platforms, and sliding panels offer physical variety that prevents the gameplay from becoming monotonous.

| Block Name | Collision Type | Height Delta | Structural Role |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Base Platform | Solid, non-slip | 0.5 units | Core foundation for all custom keys |
| Small Cube | Solid, standard | 1.0 units | Vertical elevation steps and gaps |
| Disappearing Block | Temporary solid | 0.2 units | Speed-based puzzle paths |
| Damage Brick | Semi-solid | 0.5 units | Non-lethal hazards to create tension |

<Callout type="warning" title="Collision Warning">
Ensure that your support platforms are perfectly aligned with the visual boundaries of your keyboard keys. Misaligned colliders will cause players to float in mid-air or clip through the edges, breaking the satisfying illusion.
</Callout>

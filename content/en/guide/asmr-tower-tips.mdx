<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Obstacles & Layout Optimization">

To prevent your tower from becoming monotonous, it is highly recommended to introduce advanced obstacles and layout optimizations. Incorporating dynamic elements like disappearing platforms, vertical climbs, and non-lethal hazards adds a layer of engagement without causing excessive frustration. The key is to maintain a steady difficulty curve that rewards player patience while preserving the relaxing auditory experience.

When placing obstacles, consider how they affect the overall pacing of the climb. For instance, a disappearing staircase forces players to move quickly, which can be paired with high-tempo sound blocks like the RGB keys. Conversely, static platform jumps allow players to take their time, making them a perfect match for the soothing, squishy sounds of slime blocks.

| Obstacle Type | Player Impact | Satisfying Element | Optimal Placement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disappearing Spiral | Temporary platform | High-tension rhythm | Mid-tower ascension points |
| Damage Bricks | Reduces health slightly | Visual and audio cues | Straight pathways between jumps |
| Slap Hand | Fun physics interaction | High-impact feedback | Mid-way reward checkpoints |

<Callout type="info" title="Pacing Tip">
Using non-lethal damage bricks instead of instant-kill hazards keeps the gameplay accessible and maintains a steady, satisfying progression loop for players of all skill levels.
</Callout>
